#FIMI operations' effectiveness has so far been an open empirical question, with evidence suggesting their impact might be more limited than initially assumed. That's about to change with malicious #AI swarms. These are AI agents with persistent identities and memory that would come across as a real, diverse crowd of people, and could self-coordinate and infiltrate online communities. The risk is real and imminent. To discuss the effects of AI swarms on electoral integrity, we convened a panel for the DW Global Media Forum #GMF2026, which took place this year at the World Conference Center Bonn.

JUN 2026 · LINKEDIN

Last week, I was in #Belfast for the #EPSS2026. I presented our ongoing work with Lucan Way and Steve Levitsky on how resource concentration leads to autocratic durability. We show that there is one common thread across durable #autocratic regimes that are quite diverse in institutions, wealth, and culture: whether resources are concentrated in the hands of the state. When they are, it is difficult for the #opposition to mount an independent challenge against the regime, and easier for the government to divide, demobilize, and co-opt the opposition.

#Donors pour money into #GovTech expecting a #democratization dividend. #Autocrats are spending just as eagerly on the same tools. At least 88 authoritarian regimes currently run GovTech projects. JUL 2025 · LINKEDIN

Why democracy needs business? A strong and autonomous private sector is critical to the creation of a robust opposition and an independent civil society that are central to democratic resilience. But there are risks! Even in rich, well-established democracies, governments have strong discretionary powers to help allies and punish rivals. That is why it is not business capture of the state but rather state capture of business that poses the greatest danger to democracy, we argue in our recent piece in the Journal of Democracy with Lucan Way and Steve Levitsky.
